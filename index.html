Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India • Aerospace Scientist • 11th President of India

1931 – 2015

About Him

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, came from humble beginnings. Despite financial hardships, he pursued his education with dedication. He became a renowned aerospace scientist, played a pivotal role in India’s missile development and satellite launch vehicle programs at ISRO and DRDO, and served as the 11th President of India.

Dr. Kalam’s life was guided by simplicity, discipline, and humility. He strongly believed in the power of education and the potential of youth to transform the nation. Beyond science, he authored several influential books and delivered lectures inspiring millions to dream big. Read More.

Life Journey

15 Oct 1931

Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a modest family.

1969

Joined ISRO and led the development of India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).

1983

Headed the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP).

25 Jul 2002 – 25 Jul 2007

Served as the 11th President of India, widely known as the “People’s President”.

27 Jul 2015

Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.

Scientific Contributions

Dr. Kalam was instrumental in advancing India’s space and strategic defence capabilities, contributing to self-reliance in critical aerospace technologies. He led projects that resulted in India becoming self-reliant in critical technologies like satellite launch vehicles and ballistic missiles. His dedication earned him the title “Missile Man of India.”

Achievements & Honors

Legacy

After his presidency, Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam’s legacy continued to shape India’s national and institutional landscape. His birthday 15 October, is observed as World Students’ Day in India, reflecting his lifelong commitment to youth and education. Several public institutions, roads, science centres, and research facilities have been named in his honour, including Abdul Kalam Island, India’s missile test range, symbolizing his lasting impact on the country’s strategic and scientific infrastructure.

His influence on science and education endures through awards, fellowships, and academic initiatives. Scientists have honored him by naming species such as Solibacillus kalamii and Drypetes kalamii after him. Through his books, speeches, and vision for ethical leadership, Dr. Kalam continues to inspire generations to learn, innovate, and serve society.
